The Beginning

Born from the
Hills of Nakawa

In 2018, on a quiet escarpment above Kampala's eastern horizon, a building began that would redefine what luxury hospitality means in East Africa. The founders — a family of architects and restaurateurs — had a simple and radical conviction: that a hotel should feel like the finest home you have never lived in.

We did not build Haylo to compete with the world. We built it to give Kampala something that had never existed here before — a place where silence is a luxury, and every detail is a declaration.

Every material was sourced with intention. The volcanic stone facing the western facade came from the Rwenzori foothills. The hand-woven linen was commissioned from artisans in Masindi. The garden, planted before a single room was furnished, was designed to mature with the building over twenty years. This is not a hotel that was built quickly.

A Decade of Craft

Milestones That
Shaped Our Story

From the first stone to our most recent accolade — each chapter written with the same unyielding care.

2018

The First Stone

Groundbreaking ceremony on Nakawa Hill. Construction begins with a team of 240 artisans and a founding architect's brief of six words: "Build it to last 200 years."

2019

The Garden is Planted

Twelve months before the first guest arrives, the grounds are landscaped. A head gardener from Entebbe Botanical Gardens is appointed to oversee a 20-year growth plan for the estate.

2020

Doors Open

Haylo Hotel opens its doors — quietly, without fanfare — with 18 suites and a reservation list already six months deep. The hotel's first full year ends at 96% occupancy.

2021

The Spa Opens

The 1,400m² Haylo Spa launches with a programme built on ancient Ugandan wellness traditions, reimagined for the contemporary guest. A three-year waitlist forms within days.

2022

East Africa's Best

Haylo is named Best Luxury Hotel in East Africa at the Continental Hospitality Awards — the first Ugandan property to receive the honour in the event's 22-year history.

2024

The Residences Launch

The Sky Residences — six long-stay apartments for guests who wish to remain indefinitely — open in the extended east wing. All six are reserved within 48 hours of announcement.

2026

The Story Continues

Today. With 48 suites, three restaurants, a world-class spa, and a reputation that precedes itself across four continents — Haylo's finest chapter is always the one being written now.

The Design Philosophy

Obsidian & Gold —
A Language of Restraint

Every surface at Haylo was chosen not for its cost but for its character. The deep obsidian that defines our walls is not darkness — it is a canvas onto which light performs. The gold that traces our edges is not decoration — it is the signature of a commitment to craft that runs through every detail from the architecture down to the thread count of the linen. This is a hotel that believes silence is the ultimate luxury, and that restraint — wielded with precision — is the highest form of design.

The Founder's Vision

Built to Endure
the Next Century

When we began, people asked us what kind of hotel we wanted to build. We answered: the kind that improves with time. The kind that a guest returns to twenty years later and finds more beautiful than when they left. We were not building for a market — we were building for a generation.

Our commitment to the region runs deeper than the hospitality industry. Every artisan who worked on this building was trained here. Every supplier operates within a 300km radius. Haylo's story is inseparable from Uganda's story — and we consider that the greatest privilege of all.
